The flight and handling qualities in this design were an important and critical issue. With the new fuselage plug behind the original center of gravity, the modified airplane has an afterward CG,
when comparing to the former aircraft.

This let to reducing the static margin and pilot forces to maneuver the aircraft. To do the analysis of this section, the calculations were based on the ones presented in section 6.2.4.

The first part is to determine the aerodynamic derivatives and the new CG envelope, then calculate the flight and handling qualities themselves, and finally propose modifications to maintain
these qualities similar to the original airplane, guaranteeing the aircraft stability and maneuverability, reducing further pilot excessive training when changing from ERJ145 to ERJ145G.

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textit{\textbf{Special Considerations:}}
\end{itemize}

Before showing the results of the flight an handling qualities, it is necessary to establish some assumptions to do the final calculations:

\begin{description}
	\item[a. Force Limits:] based on the pacts presented in the section 6.5.3, two main considerations regarding the minimum pilot force were used. The first one is related to the FAR 23.155. The airplane
	was calibrated to achieve the maximum load factor of 2.5 with a minimum pilot force of 50 pounds. The second regulation taken into account was the FAR 25.173b. The airplane has to return to the original
	airspeed with an error of 10 \% after a stick control. With a control system friction around 3 pound, it was considered that the force to change 10 \% in the airspeed must be, at least, this value of 3 pounds.
	\item[b. Automatic Tab Calibration:] in order to achieve the limit forces previously described, the automatic tab gain ( ) was adjusted. This calibration is to approximate de model to the real airplane and
	try to do a more precise analysis. The value of  calculate was of -0.579.
	\item[c. New CG Envelope:] in order to evaluate the flight and handling characteristics of the ERJ145G a new CG envelope was assumed. The original CG was firstly the same in its shape, but was shifted afterwards
	11 \% of the aerodynamic chord. This was necessary and sufficient to insert the Back to Forward Load (purple curve in Figure \ref{fig:passenger_load_erj145g}) into the envelope.
\end{description}

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textit{\textbf{Rotate Force:}}
\end{itemize}

The reduction in static margin could lead to a critical condition for pilot minimum force to change speed. In order to avoid this situation, the geared tab was modified to keep the minimum force within the limit prescribed by
requirements.

The geared tab modification increases pilot force for all flight conditions. Therefore the other limit must be verified. That is, the flight conditions that require the maximum pilot control forces. One critical condition is
the force required to rotate the aircraft during takeoff at the most forward cg position and MTOW.

In this analysis, the aircraft at most forward cg position was trimmed for $V_2$ with one engine inoperative. The force on the column for rotating aircraft at $V_R$ as a function of takeoff weight is shown in figure \ref{fig:force_rotate}.
